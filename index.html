I would like to preface this photo essay by saying that masculinity as a whole is not inherently toxic, nor does it lead to depression. There is healthy masculinity and there is also unhealthy masculinity. Societal pressures present boys with a narrow definition of what it means to “be a man.” Fitting all boys into a box can leave lasting damage. Rather than teaching boys how “to be a man”, perhaps society must redefine masculinity and show boys what it means to be a human.

Cal Poly San Luis Obispo has over 20,000 students enrolled with diverse life experiences. Many boys feel restricted to behave in a manner that fits the societal expectations of masculinity in college. It’s innate among humans to want to fit in. Some Cal Poly students recognize the societal expectations burdening them and are redefining masculinity in a healthy way.
